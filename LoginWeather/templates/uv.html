What is the UV Index?

The UV Index is a standard measurement of the strength of sunburn-producing ultraviolet radiation at a particular place and time. Values of the index range from zero upward - the higher the UVI value, the greater the potential for damage to the skin and eyes, and the less time it takes for harm to occur.

UV Index Scale:

  • 0-2 Low: A low risk of harm from unprotected sun exposure.
  • 3-5 Moderate: A moderate risk of harm from unprotected sun exposure.
  • 6-7 High: A high risk of harm from unprotected sun exposure. Protection against skin and eye damage is needed.
  • 8-10 Very High: Very high risk of harm from unprotected sun exposure. Take extra precautions because unprotected skin and eyes will be damaged and can burn quickly.
  • 11+ Extreme: Extreme risk of harm from unprotected sun exposure. Take all precautions because unprotected skin and eyes can burn in minutes.

It's recommended to wear protective clothing, sunglasses, and sunscreen if you need to be outside during daylight hours when UV levels are high.
