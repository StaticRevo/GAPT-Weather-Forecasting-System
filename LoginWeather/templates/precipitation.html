What is Precipitation?

Precipitation refers to any form of water - liquid or solid - that falls from the atmosphere and reaches the ground. It includes various forms such as rain, snow, sleet, and hail. Precipitation is a critical component of the water cycle and is vital for life on Earth, influencing weather patterns, climate, and the environment.

Common Forms of Precipitation:

  • Rain: Liquid water droplets that fall when atmospheric temperatures are above freezing.
  • Snow: Ice crystals that fall when atmospheric temperatures are below freezing, accumulating as solid flakes.
  • Sleet: Frozen raindrops or refrozen melted snowflakes that fall as ice pellets.
  • Hail: Hard, rounded pellets or lumps of ice that are larger and more damaging than sleet.
