Understanding Atmospheric Pressure

Atmospheric pressure is the force exerted by the weight of the air above it. This pressure is measured using a barometer and is expressed in units called hectopascals (hPa), also known as millibars. Standard atmospheric pressure at sea level is about 1013.25 hPa.

Why It Matters:

  • Weather Prediction: Changes in atmospheric pressure can indicate upcoming weather conditions. For example, falling pressure suggests worsening weather conditions, while rising pressure indicates improving weather.
  • Altitude Effects: Pressure decreases with higher altitudes which can affect breathing, boiling point of water, and aircraft operations.
  • Health and Well-being: Sudden changes in atmospheric pressure can influence human and animal behavior and health, such as joint pain or headaches in some people.
